      $H_{KS}\phi _p(\textbf{r},t)=i\hbar \frac{\partial \phi _p(\textbf{r},t)}{\partial t}$

      If we plug in the orbital exapnsion in adiabatic eigenstate

      $\phi _p(\textbf{r},t)=\sum _i a_i(t)\psi _p(\textbf{r},\textbf{R})$

      $i\hbar \frac{\partial a_i(t)}{\partial t}=a_i(t)\epsilon _i (R)-i\hbar \sum _j a_j(t)\textbf{d}_{ij}.\textbf{V}$

      Non-adiabatic coupling vectors

      $\textbf{d}_{ij}.\textbf{V} \equiv \sum _\alpha d_{ij}^\alpha \textbf{V}_\alpha \\ d_{ij}^\alpha\equiv <\psi_i|\frac{\partial \psi _j}{\partial \textbf{R}_\alpha}>$

    • Simulation

      • FIREBALL: FIREBALL is a density functional theory approach which is based on local orbitals within a nonlocal pseudopotential scheme. FIREBALL is based on a self-consistent functional.

      Optimization: we optimize the molecule for 250 fs to minimize the energy of the structure.
      Molecular Dynamics: The system is allowed to evolve at 300 K for 15000 fs using ground state MD. This step will provide us with 60000 structures of the system.
      Molecular Dynamics in Electronic Transition : 500 structures are randomly chosen from MD. After 1000 fs, virtually all structures have decayed from LUMO to HOMO. Each trajectory is then analyzed.
